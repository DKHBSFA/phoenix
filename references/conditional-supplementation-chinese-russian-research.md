# Conditional Supplementation: Chinese & Russian Research

**Date:** 2026-03-14
**Scope:** Supplements and compounds from Chinese (TCM) and Russian (sports pharmacology/adaptogen) traditions that DIFFER FROM or ADD TO the standard Western supplement stack (VitD/Omega-3/Creatine/Mg/Fe/Collagen).
**Method:** PubMed, PMC, Frontiers, ScienceDirect, Springer, Lancet eClinicalMedicine, MDPI, ResearchGate; Chinese and Russian language keyword searches cross-referenced with English-language meta-analyses.

---

## Summary: What These Traditions Add

| Tradition | Core Principle | Key Difference from Western Approach |
|-----------|---------------|--------------------------------------|
| **Russian** | Adaptogens — non-specific stress resistance | Whole-plant extracts that modulate HPA axis, not single nutrients |
| **Chinese** | TCM — systemic balance, qi/blood tonics | Multi-compound formulas targeting telomeres, mitochondria, immune modulation |
| **Both** | Conditional use based on stress/load/season | Not "take daily forever" — cycled based on training phase |

## TIER 1: Strong Clinical Evidence (RCTs, Meta-Analyses)

### 1. Rhodiola rosea (Родиола розовая / 红景天)

**Origin:** Soviet military/cosmonaut research (Brekhman, 1960s-1980s). Used by MIR station cosmonauts, Siberian soldiers, Olympic athletes.

**Mechanism:** Modulates cortisol response, increases ERK1/2 phosphorylation, enhances mitochondrial ATP production. Reduces perceived fatigue via serotonin/dopamine pathway modulation.

**Clinical Evidence:**
| Study | Dose | Duration | Population | Result |
|-------|------|----------|------------|--------|
| De Bock 2004 | 200 mg (3% rosavin, 1% salidroside) | Acute | 24 active adults | Increased time-to-exhaustion |
| Noreen 2013 | ~170 mg (3 mg/kg) | Acute, 60 min pre | 18 active females | Improved 6-mile cycling TT |
| Ballmann 2019 | 1500 mg/d x 3d + 500 mg pre | 4 days | 11 active females | Enhanced Wingate mean/peak power |
| Liu 2023 | 2400 mg/d | 30 days | 48 untrained males | Bench +9%, squat +7.5% 1RM |
| Skarpanska-Stejnborn 2009 | 200 mg/d | 28 days | 22 elite rowers | NO effect on 2000m rowing |
| Shanely 2014 | 600 mg/d | 38 days | 48 marathon runners | NO effect on race time |

**Key Pattern:** Works best in recreationally active individuals, NOT elite athletes. Higher doses (1500-2400 mg) needed for strength; lower doses (170-200 mg acute) sufficient for endurance.

**Recommended Protocol:**
- **Endurance days:** 200 mg, 60 min pre-exercise (standardized 3% rosavin, 1% salidroside)
- **Strength blocks:** 600-1500 mg/d for up to 4 weeks, then cycle off
- **Anti-fatigue/cognitive:** 200-400 mg/d
- **Cycle:** 4 weeks on, 2 weeks off (Soviet protocol)

**What it adds to Western stack:** Nothing in the standard VitD/Omega-3/Creatine list addresses HPA axis modulation or perceived fatigue. Rhodiola fills this gap.

### 2. Astragalus membranaceus / Cycloastragenol (黄芪)

**Origin:** TCM "qi tonic" used 2000+ years. Modern research on telomerase activation (TA-65 compound).

**Mechanism:** Cycloastragenol and astragaloside IV activate telomerase, lengthening telomeres. Also: immunomodulatory, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, hepatoprotective.

**Clinical Evidence:**
| Study | Compound | Dose | Duration | Result |
|-------|----------|------|----------|--------|
| RCT 2024 (MDPI Nutrients) | ASTCOQ02 | 250 mg astragalus + 40 mg astragaloside IV + 25 mg cycloastragenol | 6 months | Significant telomere lengthening |
| TA-65 meta-analysis (8 RCTs, n=750) | TA-65 | 250-500 Units/d | 12 months | Moderate telomere elongation, amplified in adults >60 |
| Harley 2011 | TA-65 | 250 Units/d | 12 months | Significant telomere lengthening vs placebo (placebo lost length) |
| Multiple RCTs | TA-65 | Various | Various | Decreased immunosenescent CD8+CD28- T cells |

**Safety:** GRAS status. No significant adverse effects in trials up to 12 months.

**Recommended Protocol:**
- **Telomere maintenance:** 250-500 mg astragalus extract daily (standardized to astragaloside IV)
- **Or:** TA-65 250 Units/d (more expensive, more targeted)
- **Minimum duration:** 6 months for measurable telomere effect

**What it adds to Western stack:** UNIQUE — no Western supplement directly activates telomerase. This is the strongest longevity-specific compound from either tradition with RCT backing.

### 3. Cordyceps sinensis / militaris (冬虫夏草)

**Origin:** TCM kidney/lung tonic. Chinese athletes' "secret weapon" (1993 Chinese National Games controversy). Extensively studied at Chinese universities.

**Mechanism:** Cordycepin (3'-deoxyadenosine) increases ATP production, enhances oxygen utilization. Polysaccharides modulate immune function.

**Clinical Evidence:**
| Study | Dose | Duration | Population | Result |
|-------|------|----------|------------|--------|
| Cs-4 RCT (Chinese J Integrative Med) | 3 g/d | 6 weeks | 37 elderly Chinese | VO2max and anaerobic threshold significantly increased |
| Cs-4 RCT #2 | 3 g/d | 12 weeks | Healthy elderly | Metabolic threshold +10.5%, ventilatory threshold +8.5% |
| C. militaris RCT (JISSN 2017) | 4 g/d | 3 weeks | 28 healthy adults | Improved tolerance to high-intensity exercise |
| Amateur marathoners RCT | Various | 12 weeks | Marathoners | Mixed results |
| Meta-analysis (Frontiers 2025) | Various | Various | Multiple | Significant improvement in VO2peak (p=0.04), ventilatory threshold (p=0.03) |

**Dose-Response:** 3 g/d minimum for VO2max effects. 1 g/d insufficient except with 12+ weeks. Higher doses = faster onset.

**Recommended Protocol:**
- **Aerobic performance:** Cs-4 extract 3 g/d, minimum 6 weeks
- **General health/immune:** 1-1.5 g/d ongoing
- **Note:** Cs-4 (fermented mycelium) is the most clinically validated form. Wild cordyceps is unsustainable and often contaminated.

**What it adds to Western stack:** Direct VO2max enhancement mechanism distinct from creatine (which targets phosphocreatine system). Complementary, not overlapping.

### 4. Ecdysterone / Leuzea (Левзея / Rhaponticum carthamoides)

**Origin:** Russian sports pharmacology. Rhaponticum carthamoides (maral root) from Altai/Saian mountains. Soviet athletes used it extensively.

**Mechanism:** 20-Hydroxyecdysone (20HE) binds estrogen receptor beta (ERbeta), activating PI3K/Akt pathway for protein synthesis WITHOUT androgenic effects. No hormonal suppression.

**Clinical Evidence:**
| Study | Dose | Duration | Population | Result |
|-------|------|----------|------------|--------|
| Isenmann 2019 (Free Univ Berlin) | 200 mg and 800 mg/d | 10 weeks | Resistance-trained males | Increased body weight, muscle mass, strength/power |
| BIO101 Phase II trials | 350-900 mg/d | Various | Multiple populations | Well tolerated, dose-dependent effects |
| Russian athletic studies | Extract doses | Various | Elite athletes | Increased muscle mass, decreased fat mass during training |

**Safety:** LD50 >9 g/kg orally in mice. No serious adverse effects in human trials. NOT on WADA banned list (as of 2024, though under monitoring).

**Recommended Protocol:**
- **Muscle/strength phase:** 200-800 mg ecdysterone/d (from Rhaponticum carthamoides extract)
- **Or:** Spinach-derived 20HE at 1g/kg body weight daily (food-based approach)
- **Duration:** 8-12 week cycles

**What it adds to Western stack:** Natural anabolic that does not suppress endogenous hormones. No equivalent in Western supplement lists. Russian research base spans 50+ years.

## TIER 2: Moderate Evidence (Multiple Studies, Some RCTs)

### 5. Schisandra chinensis (Лимонник / 五味子)

**Origin:** Both Russian and Chinese traditions. Part of the Soviet "adaptogen triad" (Rhodiola + Eleuthero + Schisandra). TCM "five-flavor berry."

**Mechanism:** Lignans (schisandrins A/B/C) — hepatoprotective, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory. Suppresses cortisol and nitric oxide spikes from intense exertion. Modulates phosphorylated stress-activated protein kinase.

**Clinical Evidence:**
- RCT 2020: 1000 mg extract/d x 12 weeks → significant quadriceps strength improvement, reduced resting lactate
- RCT 2021: Schisandra + low-intensity exercise → improved muscle mass in older adults
- Panossian 2021: Modulates cortisol response comparable to Rhodiola
- In China: clinically used medications for drug-induced liver injury (reduces ALT, AST, bilirubin)

**Recommended Protocol:**
- **Adaptogen/recovery:** 500-1000 mg standardized extract/d
- **Liver protection (heavy training):** 1000-2000 mg/d
- **Powder:** 1.5-6 g/d
- **Best combined** with Rhodiola and/or Eleuthero (Soviet triad)

**What it adds:** Unique liver-protective function relevant to athletes. No Western supplement addresses exercise-induced hepatic stress specifically.

### 6. Reishi / Lingzhi (灵芝 / Ganoderma lucidum)

**Origin:** TCM "mushroom of immortality." 2000+ years of documented use. 400+ bioactive compounds identified.

**Mechanism:** Beta-glucan polysaccharides activate macrophages and NK cells. Triterpenoids: anti-inflammatory, hepatoprotective, antihypertensive. Unique bidirectional immune modulation (upregulates when suppressed, downregulates when overactive).

**Clinical Evidence:**
- Chinese Pharmacopoeia recommends 6-12 g extract daily
- Ganopoly (polysaccharide extract) up to 5.4 g/d x 12 weeks in clinical trials
- Immune modulation demonstrated in multiple studies
- Limited RCT data specifically for athletic performance or longevity endpoints

**Recommended Protocol:**
- **Immune modulation:** 1.5-5 g extract/d (standardized to polysaccharides)
- **Chinese Pharmacopoeia standard:** 6-12 g/d
- **Best form:** Dual extraction (hot water + ethanol) to capture both polysaccharides and triterpenoids

**What it adds:** Bidirectional immune modulation is unique — prevents both immunosuppression from overtraining and excessive inflammation. No Western supplement does this.

---

### 7. Panax Ginseng / Ginsenosides (人参)

**Origin:** Chinese/Korean. Most-researched adaptogen globally. Ginsenosides are the active compounds (>30 identified).

**Mechanism:** Ginsenosides activate SIRT1 pathway (longevity pathway), enhance mitochondrial function, reduce oxidative stress. Anti-inflammatory via NF-kB modulation.

**Anti-Aging Evidence (Strong):**
- Activates SIRT1 pathway (same pathway as caloric restriction/fasting)
- Enhances mitochondrial function and cellular resilience
- Telomerase activity increase (preclinical)
- Antioxidant enzyme upregulation (GSH-Px, SOD)

**Athletic Performance Evidence (Weak):**
- Meta-analysis: does NOT reliably enhance physical performance
- High doses >300 mg/d showed some short-term vertical jump benefit only
- Fatigue reduction: 400-3000 mg/d effective for reducing perceived fatigue

**Recommended Protocol:**
- **Anti-aging/longevity:** 200-400 mg/d standardized extract (4-7% ginsenosides)
- **Anti-fatigue:** 400 mg/d
- **NOT recommended for:** direct athletic performance enhancement
- **Cycle:** 2-3 months on, 1 month off (TCM tradition)

**What it adds:** SIRT1 activation parallels fasting benefits — synergistic with Phoenix fasting protocol. Not a performance supplement but a longevity compound.

## TIER 3: Emerging Evidence (Preclinical Strong, Clinical Limited)

### 8. Goji Berry / Lycium barbarum Polysaccharides (枸杞)

**Origin:** TCM "longevity fruit." Ningxia province primary growing region.

**Mechanism:** LBPs (Lycium barbarum polysaccharides) — antioxidant, immune-enhancing, gut microbiome modulation, anti-inflammatory. Zeaxanthin content highest of any known food (eye health).

**Evidence:**
- Multiple in vitro/in vivo studies showing lifespan extension (C. elegans)
- Clinical studies show improved general wellbeing and immune function
- Anti-aging validated across broad study range but human longevity trials absent
- Gut microbiome effects increasingly documented

**Recommended Protocol:**
- **Dried berries:** 15-30 g/d (traditional dose)
- **Extract:** 500-2000 mg LBP/d
- **Better as food than supplement** — eaten in Phoenix nutrition protocol directly

**Status:** Strong preclinical, limited clinical. Better integrated as food (already in Phoenix food database) than as isolated supplement.

---

### 9. Eleuthero / Siberian Ginseng (Элеутерококк)

**Origin:** Brekhman's primary research subject. 1000+ Soviet studies (most untranslated). Used by cosmonauts, soldiers, submarine crews.

**Mechanism:** Eleutherosides modulate stress response. Historically claimed to improve endurance, immune function, cognitive performance under stress.

**Clinical Evidence — CONFLICTING:**
- WHO recommends 2-3 g dried root/d
- 800 mg/d x 8 weeks: improved physical performance in one study
- BUT: 5 rigorous studies found NO benefit at 1000-1200 mg/d for 1-6 weeks
- One study found it INCREASED stress hormone ratios (testosterone:cortisol worsened)
- 3 positive studies all had "severe methodological flaws" per systematic review

**Assessment:** The most overhyped adaptogen. Soviet research was largely uncontrolled. Modern rigorous trials do not support the historical claims. WADA considered banning it but evidence of benefit was too weak to justify.

**Recommendation:** NOT recommended as a standalone supplement. If used, only as part of the Soviet triad formula with Rhodiola + Schisandra (where Rhodiola does the heavy lifting).

---

### 10. Meldonium (Мельдоний / Mildronate)

**Origin:** Developed 1970s by Latvian chemist Ivars Kalvins at Latvian Institute of Organic Synthesis. Widely used in Russian/Eastern European sports and cardiology.

**Mechanism:** Inhibits carnitine biosynthesis → shifts metabolism from fatty acid beta-oxidation to glucose oxidation → more efficient oxygen use. Cardioprotective.

**Clinical Evidence:**
- Increases endurance performance (mechanism confirmed)
- Cardioprotective in athletes with "athletic heart syndrome"
- Reduces exercise-induced inflammation and ROS
- Enhances CNS activation

**Status:** WADA BANNED since January 1, 2016. Cannot be used in competitive sport.

**Relevance to Phoenix:** The mechanism (metabolic shift to carbohydrate oxidation under hypoxic stress) is interesting for understanding conditioning, but the compound itself is not usable. The metabolic principle can be partially achieved through training periodization and fasting protocols.

## CHINA-SPECIFIC FINDINGS

### Vitamin D Deficiency in Chinese Populations

**Critical finding** that differs from Western assumptions:

| Finding | Data |
|---------|------|
| Overall VitD deficiency (<20 ng/mL) | 29.89% of Chinese population |
| Overall VitD inadequacy (<30 ng/mL) | **63.2%** of Chinese adults |
| Northern China deficiency | 44.02% |
| Females | 39.95% |
| Under 40 years | 33.46% |
| Urban residents | 39.44% |

**Dose-Response RCT (Chinese population):**
| Dose | 25(OH)D increase at 16 weeks | % reversing deficiency |
|------|----------------------------|-----------------------|
| 400 IU/d | +21.7 nmol/L | 53% |
| 800 IU/d | +26.3 nmol/L | 67% |
| 1200 IU/d | +32.0 nmol/L | 77% |
| 2000 IU/d | +36.3 nmol/L | 80% |

**Key finding:** Even 2000 IU/d did NOT achieve 25(OH)D >= 50 nmol/L in 97.5% of Chinese subjects. This contrasts with Western guidelines where 2000 IU/d is often considered sufficient. Chinese populations may need higher doses due to dietary patterns, skin pigmentation, and indoor lifestyle prevalence.

## CoQ10 — Cross-Cultural Note

- Chinese athlete study: No significant hepatic mitochondrial improvement from CoQ10 supplementation in adolescent athletes
- International evidence: CoQ10 + HIIT in older adults (65-75) → greater lower-body strength/power vs HIIT alone
- Systematic review: Reduces fatigue markers, improves anaerobic performance
- **Assessment:** Evidence supports CoQ10 for older adults combining with exercise, but NOT as a standalone anti-aging supplement. Already in many Western protocols.

## Key Principles from Both Traditions

1. **Cycling, not chronic use:** Both Russian and Chinese traditions emphasize cycling supplements (4 weeks on / 2 weeks off, or seasonal). This contrasts with Western "take daily indefinitely" approach.

2. **Adaptogens work best under stress:** Evidence consistently shows adaptogens are most effective during high training loads, sleep deprivation, or psychological stress. Less benefit at baseline.

3. **Combinatorial formulas over single compounds:** The Soviet triad (Rhodiola + Schisandra + Eleuthero) and TCM multi-herb formulas reflect a systems approach. Single-compound studies may underestimate real-world effects of traditional combinations.

4. **Conditional, not universal:** These are not "everyone should take these" supplements. They are conditional on training phase, stress level, season, and individual response.
