## 1. Optimal TRE Window

### Duration: 8 hours is the evidence sweet spot

The most studied TRE protocol is TRE-8 (8-hour eating window / 16-hour fast). A 2025 Frontiers review concluded: "TRE-8 has received much attention, with existing evidence indicating its advantages in clinical applications" [1]. Protocols ranging from TRE-4 to TRE-12 have been tested, but 8 hours balances metabolic benefits with practical adherence.

Satchin Panda's foundational 2012 mouse study demonstrated that mice on a high-fat diet eating within an 8-hour window did not gain weight and showed multiple health improvements versus ad libitum feeding of the same diet [2]. Panda's subsequent work confirmed that "consuming all calories within a consistent 8-12 hours can sustain daily rhythms in anabolic and catabolic metabolism" [2].

Valter Longo recommends a 12-hour window (e.g. 08:00-20:00) with no food 3-4 hours before bed [3]. This is more conservative than most TRE research but reflects his emphasis on sustainability and compliance.

### Timing: Early TRE (eTRE) is superior

A 2025 3-month RCT comparing early TRE (eTRE, eating window ~08:00-16:00) vs late TRE (lTRE, eating window ~12:00-20:00) with energy restriction found that eTRE produced superior outcomes on:
- Body fat mass reduction
- Diastolic blood pressure
- Metabolic age
- Fasting glucose

[4] DOI: 10.1016/j.clnu.2025.02.018 (Clinical Nutrition, 2025)

However, a 2025 study (ChronoFast trial) under **isocaloric** conditions found neither early (08:00-16:00) nor late (13:00-21:00) TRE produced clinically meaningful cardiometabolic improvements [5]. This suggests TRE timing benefits may be partly mediated by spontaneous caloric reduction in earlier windows.

A Drosophila study (Ulgherait et al., 2022) demonstrated that night-biased fasting (fasting aligned with the organism's rest period) was essential for longevity benefits. Daytime fasting provided no benefit. "Night-specific induction of autophagy is both necessary and sufficient for iTRF-mediated health benefits" [6].

**Translating to humans:** The rest period = nighttime. Therefore fasting should overlap maximally with the night. This supports early eating windows (e.g. 08:00-16:00 or 09:00-17:00) rather than late ones (14:00-22:00).

### Consensus

| Source | Recommended Window | Duration |
|--------|--------------------|----------|
| Frontiers review 2025 [1] | 8h, early preferred | 8h |
| Panda lab [2] | 8-12h, consistent daily | 8-12h |
| Longo [3] | 08:00-20:00, 3-4h before bed stop | 12h |
| Clinical Nutrition RCT 2025 [4] | ~08:00-16:00 (early) | 8h |
| Ulgherait et al. 2022 [6] | Night-biased fasting (early eating) | -- |

**Verdict:** 8-10 hour window, starting no later than 09:00, ending by 17:00-19:00.

## 2. Autophagy Activation and Timing

### When does autophagy activate?

- **12-16 hours** after last food intake: liver glycogen depletes, AMPK activates, mTOR is suppressed, autophagy initiates [1][7]
- **16-24 hours:** autophagy intensifies, ketone body production rises [7]
- **24-48 hours:** glycogen fully depleted, maximal autophagy activation [7]

Russian clinical sources confirm: "Autophagy activates approximately 12-16 hours after the last meal and actively continues up to 36-48 hours" [8].

### Circadian regulation of autophagy

Autophagy is not constant -- it follows circadian rhythms. Key autophagy genes (Atg1, Atg8a) show peak expression during the nighttime/fasting period [6]. This means the fasting period should align with night for maximal autophagy.

### The first human autophagy measurement in TRE (2025)

Bensalem et al. (2025) conducted a landmark 6-month RCT (n=121, people with obesity) comparing:
- Intermittent TRE (iTRE)
- Caloric restriction (CR)
- Standard care control

They measured **autophagic flux** (LC3B-II in PBMCs) -- the first time this was done in humans during a dietary intervention. Result: iTRE showed significantly higher autophagic flux vs control at 6 months (P=0.04) [9].

DOI: 10.1113/JP287938

### FMD and autophagy (2025 human trial)

A 2025 trial (n=30) of the Fasting Mimicking Diet (ProLon) found increased autophagic flux by day 6 that **persisted even after two days of normal eating**. Critically, a modified version (FMD2) with different macronutrient ratios showed autophagy effects that faded after refeeding, demonstrating that "nutrient composition matters, not just calorie reduction" [10].

## 3. Meal Structure Within TRE

### How many meals?

Longo recommends [3]:
- **Overweight individuals:** 2 meals + 2 snacks (<100 kcal, <5g sugar each)
- **Normal weight or 65+:** 3 meals + 1 snack

For the Phoenix context (96kg, active, longevity focus): **3 meals within 8-10 hours** is optimal, balancing nutrient absorption, protein distribution, and practical compliance.

### Macronutrient framework

Chinese nutritional guidelines (2024-2025) recommend the classic 3:4:3 caloric distribution across 3 meals (30% breakfast, 40% lunch, 30% dinner) with macros of fat 20-30%, protein 15-20%, carbs 50-60% [11].

Longo's Longevity Diet emphasizes [3]:
- Primarily plant-based with fish 2-3x/week
- Legumes as primary protein
- Olive oil (3 tbsp/day), nuts (1 oz/day)
- Minimize saturated fats and sugar
- Complex carbohydrates over simple ones

## 4. Caloric Considerations

### Should calories be reduced for longevity?

**Yes, moderate reduction is beneficial.** The CALERIE trial (2-year RCT, n=199) targeted 25% CR but participants achieved ~12% on average. Even at this modest level [12]:
- 9 senescence biomarkers reduced at 12 months (IL7, MMP1, MPO, PAI1, PARC, RANTES, TARC, TNFR1, VEGF)
- 6 remained reduced at 24 months
- Pace of aging slowed (DunedinPACE DNA methylation)

DOI: 10.1111/acel.14038

**Chinese research (2024)** confirms: 40% caloric restriction produced the strongest lifespan extension in mice. However, a Google/Calico study (published in Nature) warned that extreme CR may "extend lifespan but at the cost of health parameters" [13].

### Practical target

TRE produces **spontaneous caloric reduction of ~20%** without explicit counting [1]. For a 96kg active male:
- Maintenance: ~2600-2800 kcal (adjusted from the original 2950 which included a 15-hour eating window)
- TRE target: ~2100-2400 kcal (modest 10-15% reduction via natural compression)
- No need to count obsessively -- the time restriction itself reduces intake

### FMD cycling

Longo's FMD (5 days/month of ~1100 kcal, low protein, low sugar, high unsaturated fat) reduced biological age by 2.5 years over 3 cycles [14]. This is complementary to daily TRE, not a replacement.

## 5. Protein: The Longevity Paradox

This is the most critical and counter-intuitive finding. **Longevity-optimal protein intake is LOWER than bodybuilding recommendations.**

### Longo's protein recommendations [3]

| Age | Protein (g/kg/day) | For 96kg person |
|-----|---------------------|-----------------|
| <65 | 0.68-0.79 | 65-76g/day |
| 65+ | ~1.0 | 96g/day |

**Rationale:** High protein activates mTOR and IGF-1, which promote growth but also accelerate aging. Longo's research shows cancer rates increase ~400% among Americans getting 20%+ daily calories from protein vs those at 10% [3].

### The mTOR-autophagy tradeoff

mTOR is a master switch:
- **High mTOR** (high protein, high insulin) = protein synthesis ON, autophagy OFF
- **Low mTOR** (fasting, low protein) = protein synthesis reduced, autophagy ON

For longevity, the goal is to cycle between these states rather than keep mTOR permanently elevated (as bodybuilding diets do).

### Protein distribution within TRE

Russian sources recommend 25-35g protein per main meal, without overloading evening meals [8]. This aligns with muscle protein synthesis research showing ~0.4g/kg per meal maximizes acute MPS response.

For 96kg at 0.75 g/kg = ~72g/day across 3 meals = **~24g per meal**.

### Protein sources for longevity

Longo strongly recommends [3]:
- **Primary:** Legumes (beans, chickpeas, lentils), nuts, seeds
- **Secondary:** Fish 2-3x/week (salmon, sardines, anchovies, cod)
- **Minimize:** Red meat, processed meat
- **Occasional:** Eggs, goat/sheep dairy

## 6. First Meal and Last Meal

### First meal (breaking the fast)

The refeeding meal matters because it determines how quickly autophagy shuts down. Research on FMD refeeding and general fasting protocols suggests [10][15]:

**Ideal first meal composition:**
- Start gentle: cooked vegetables, bone broth, or light soup
- Include healthy fats (avocado, olive oil) -- these have minimal insulin/mTOR impact
- Moderate complex carbohydrates (whole grains, legumes)
- Moderate protein (not a protein bomb -- this would spike mTOR too aggressively)
- **Avoid:** refined sugars, heavy carbs, raw cruciferous vegetables (bloating)

**Rationale:** A high-protein, high-sugar first meal maximally suppresses the autophagy that accumulated overnight. A gentler refeeding extends the autophagy-to-growth transition.

### Last meal

- Must end **3-4 hours before bed** [3]
- Should be **lighter** than lunch (Chinese 3:4:3 model supports this)
- Can include the day's fish/protein portion
- Complex carbs acceptable (may aid sleep via tryptophan pathway)
- **No sugary desserts** -- insulin spike before bed disrupts overnight autophagy onset

### Meal timing example (8h window, 09:00-17:00)

| Time | Meal | Focus |
|------|------|-------|
| 09:00 | Breakfast | Gentle refeeding: cooked vegetables, olive oil, legumes, nuts, small portion whole grains |
| 13:00 | Lunch (largest) | Main protein source (fish/legumes), complex carbs, abundant vegetables |
| 16:30 | Early dinner (lighter) | Vegetables, healthy fats, moderate protein, complex carbs |

## 7. Spermidine and the Autophagy Cascade

A landmark 2024 Nature Cell Biology study (Eisenberg, Hofer, Madeo et al.) revealed that **spermidine is essential for fasting-mediated autophagy** [16].

DOI: 10.1038/s41556-024-01468-x

### Mechanism

Fasting triggers endogenous spermidine synthesis, which activates:
1. **Spermidine** rises during fasting
2. **eIF5A hypusination** (spermidine-dependent modification)
3. **TFEB translation** increases (transcription factor for autophagy genes)
4. **Autophagic flux** increases

Blocking spermidine synthesis abolished fasting's lifespan and healthspan benefits in yeast, nematodes, flies, mice, and human cells.

### Dietary implications

Spermidine-rich foods may enhance TRE's autophagy benefits:
- **High spermidine:** Wheat germ, aged cheese, mushrooms, soy products, legumes, whole grains
- **Moderate:** Green peas, broccoli, cauliflower, nuts

A 2024 Autophagy study confirmed that rapamycin-induced autophagy also requires endogenous spermidine surges [17]. DOI: 10.1080/15548627.2024.2396793

## 8. Exercise-Autophagy Interaction

### Exercise induces autophagy

Aerobic exercise activates AMPK, which promotes autophagy in skeletal muscle. A 2024 Aging and Disease review confirmed that "life-long aerobic exercise is a non-pharmacological approach for inducing autophagy and delaying muscle atrophy in the aging population" [18].

### The timing question

Exercise during the fasting period (before the eating window) would theoretically maximize autophagy via dual AMPK activation (fasting + exercise). However, this must be balanced against performance and recovery needs.

### mTOR cycling for longevity + muscle preservation

The key insight: **cycle between autophagy (fasting + exercise) and growth (refeeding with protein)**:
- **Morning fasted training** → maximizes autophagy, cellular cleanup
- **Post-workout first meal** → breaks fast, activates mTOR for repair/synthesis
- **Moderate protein at each meal** → sufficient for repair but not chronic mTOR elevation

This cycling mirrors the evolutionary pattern: hunt (fasting + exertion) then feast (refeeding).

### Chinese research note

Zhongshan University (2024) found that moderate-intensity physical activity increased longevity probability by 56%, while high-intensity activity increased it by 66%. However, excessive vigorous exercise (>1h/day intense) **reduced** longevity by 17% per additional hour [19]. This supports Phoenix's calisthenic approach over extreme training.

## 9. Chinese Literature Findings

### Key findings from Chinese sources (2024-2025)

1. **TRE definition and efficacy:** Chinese clinical guidelines define TRE as "compressing eating time within a 24-hour cycle to 10 hours or less, increasing fasting time." Cell Metabolism (2024 review) confirmed TRE as effective for weight management in overweight individuals [20].

2. **Gut microbiome synchronization:** TRE synchronizes gut microbiota rhythms with host circadian clock. During fasting periods, gut bacteria shift toward barrier maintenance and repair functions. During eating windows, they process nutrients more efficiently, producing "timely metabolic signals" [21].

3. **Akkermansia muciniphila:** The 16/8 protocol significantly increases this bacteria, which is associated with better metabolic health and longevity [22].

4. **Macronutrient distribution:** Chinese nutritional standards recommend fat 20-30%, protein 15-20%, carbs 50-60% with 3:4:3 meal calorie distribution (30% breakfast, 40% lunch, 30% dinner) [11].

5. **Caloric restriction data:** 40% CR produced strongest lifespan extension in mouse models but may come with health trade-offs. Moderate CR (10-20%) is recommended for sustainable practice [13].

## 10. Russian Literature Findings

### Key findings from Russian sources (2024-2025)

1. **Autophagy activation timeline:** Russian clinical review confirms autophagy initiates at 12-16h fasting, peaks at 24-48h. For 16/8 protocol, autophagy is "activated but may be insufficient for strong activation in some individuals." OMAD (23:1) reaches ketosis faster and maintains lower insulin longer [7][8].

2. **Sirtuin activation:** 16/8 fasting activates longevity genes, particularly sirtuins -- proteins regulating cellular health, inflammation, and metabolism [22].

3. **Protein recommendations:** Russian sources recommend 25-35g protein per main meal, distributed evenly, without overloading evening hours. Including amino acid sources with carbohydrate meals reduces subsequent hunger and stabilizes appetite [8].

4. **FMD protocol (Russian perspective):** The Valter Longo FMD protocol is well-documented in Russian clinical literature. Day 1: ~54% of normal calories (10% protein, 56% fat, 34% carbs). Days 2-5: further reduction. 3 cycles reduced biological age by 2.5 years [23].

5. **Key caution:** Russian medical sources emphasize that autophagy activation depends on the fasting period itself, not the specific diet composition during eating windows. However, food quality and protein adequacy during eating windows remain important for utilizing autophagy benefits [7].

## 11. Synthesis and Recommendation for Phoenix

### Evidence-based TRE protocol for Phoenix app

Based on trilingual research synthesis across 23+ sources:

#### Window
- **Duration:** 8-10 hours
- **Timing:** 08:00/09:00 to 16:00/17:00 (early TRE)
- **Fasting period:** 14-16 hours (overnight + morning)
- **Stop eating:** 3-4 hours before bed

#### Meals
- **3 meals** within the window (no snacking between)
- **Calorie distribution:** 30% / 40% / 30% (breakfast / lunch / dinner)
- **No calorie counting required** -- TRE naturally reduces intake ~15-20%

#### Approximate daily targets (96kg active male, longevity focus)

| Nutrient | Target | Rationale |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| Calories | ~2100-2400 kcal | Spontaneous ~15% reduction from maintenance via TRE |
| Protein | 65-80g (0.68-0.83 g/kg) | Longo longevity range; cycles mTOR rather than keeping it elevated |
| Fat | 70-90g (30-35% of kcal) | Healthy fats (olive oil, nuts, fish) |
| Carbs | 260-330g (50-55% of kcal) | Complex carbs, legumes, whole grains |
| Fiber | 30-40g | Legumes, vegetables, whole grains |

#### Meal structure

**Meal 1 -- Breakfast (09:00) -- ~630-720 kcal**
Breaking the overnight fast gently:
- Cooked vegetables (not raw cruciferous)
- Olive oil (1 tbsp)
- Whole grains (oats, whole bread)
- Nuts/seeds (walnuts, almonds -- spermidine source)
- Small legume portion (hummus, lentils)
- Protein: ~20-25g (from legumes + nuts)

**Meal 2 -- Lunch (13:00) -- ~840-960 kcal (largest meal)**
Main protein and calorie load:
- Fish (2-3x/week: salmon, sardines, anchovies) or legumes
- Abundant vegetables (varied colors)
- Complex carbs (brown rice, quinoa, sweet potato)
- Olive oil (1 tbsp)
- Protein: ~25-30g

**Meal 3 -- Early dinner (16:30) -- ~630-720 kcal**
Lighter, to allow 3-4h before bed:
- Vegetables (cooked, mixed)
- Moderate legumes or eggs
- Healthy fats (avocado, olive oil)
- Complex carbs (moderate portion)
- Protein: ~20-25g

#### Weekly additions
- **Fish:** 2-3 meals/week (lunch slot)
- **Spermidine-rich foods daily:** wheat germ, mushrooms, legumes, aged cheese
- **FMD cycles (optional):** 5-day modified fast once/month for deep autophagy reset

#### Exercise integration
- **Train fasted** (morning, before Meal 1) when possible -- maximizes autophagy
- **First meal post-workout** acts as recovery nutrition
- **Moderate intensity** preferred over extreme (longevity benefit, Chinese research)
- Training volume/intensity stays within Phoenix protocol parameters

#### What changed from the original 7-meal plan

| Original (7 meals, 07:00-22:00) | Phoenix TRE (3 meals, 09:00-17:00) |
|----------------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| 15h eating window | 8h eating window |
| ~2950 kcal | ~2100-2400 kcal |
| 7 meals + supplements spread | 3 meals, no snacking |
| High protein (~1.5-2.0 g/kg est.) | Moderate protein (0.7-0.8 g/kg) |
| Eating until 22:00 | Stop by 17:00 (3-4h before bed) |
| Minimal fasting window | 16h fasting (autophagy activation) |
| Bodybuilding-oriented | Longevity-oriented (mTOR cycling) |
